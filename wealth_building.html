Reinvesting


  Reinvesting refers to the act of taking profits earned from an investment and using them to purchase additional investments, instead of withdrawing the profits as cash. Reinvesting offers compounded growth.Reinvesting is often used as a strategy for long-term growth, as it allows investors to compound their returns by reinvesting profits instead of simply taking them as cash. It can also help investors diversify their portfolio and take advantage of new investment opportunities as they arise.

Money is not a static thing it needs channelise . A proper investment can produce exponential returns . A British-American economist "Benjamin Graham" who is also known as "Father of Investing" once said "Successful investing is about managing risk.not avoiding it".

Financial Risks


   Financial risks should be considered before invsting , you need to play according to your goals .Basically financial risk in investing refers to the possibility that an investment will result in a financial loss or a lower-than-expected return.

Some of the common financial risks one may incounter are

1.Market risk:Market risk refers to the risk that the value of an investment will decline due to changes in the overall market or economy. For example, a stock market crash could cause the value of a stock portfolio to plummet.

2.Interest rate risk:Interest rate risk refers to the risk that changes in interest rates will impact the value of an investment. For example, if interest rates rise, the value of a bond portfolio may decline.

3.Inflation risk: Inflation risk refers to the risk that the rate of inflation will outpace the returns on an investment, resulting in a loss of purchasing power.
