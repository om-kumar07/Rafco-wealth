Tax Reduction

Ways to save taxes


Invest in tax-saving schemes


     Investing in tax-saving schemes is one of the most popular ways to save taxes in India.Some tax saving schemes are:

1.Public Provident Fund (PPF):PPF is a long-term investment scheme with a lock-in period of 15 years. It is a low-risk, fixed-income investment option, and the returns are tax-free. You can invest up to Rs. 1.5 lakh per year in PPF and claim a tax deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

2.National Pension System (NPS):NPS is a retirement-oriented investment scheme that offers market-linked returns. You can invest up to Rs. 2 lakh per year in NPS and claim a tax deduction under Section 80C and Section 80CCD(1B) of the Income Tax Act.

3.Equity Linked Saving Scheme (ELSS):ELSS is a tax-saving mutual fund scheme that invests primarily in equities. It has a lock-in period of 3 years, and the returns are subject to capital gains tax. You can invest up to Rs. 1.5 lakh per year in ELSS and claim a tax deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.


4.Tax-Saving Fixed Deposits (FD):Tax-Saving FDs are fixed deposits with a lock-in period of 5 years. The interest earned is taxable, and you can invest up to Rs. 1.5 lakh per year in Tax-Saving FDs and claim a tax deduction under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act.

5.Unit-Linked Insurance Plan (ULIP):ULIP is a market-linked investment-cum-insurance plan that offers tax benefits. The premium paid towards ULIP is eligible for a tax deduction under Section 80C, and the returns are tax-free.


Health Insurance


     Having health insurance not only provides financial protection in case of medical emergencies but also offers tax benefits in India. Here are some details about tax benefits related to health insurance:

1.Tax deduction under Section 80D:You can claim a tax deduction for the premium paid towards health insurance under Section 80D of the Income Tax Act. The deduction amount depends on the type of policy and the age of the policyholder.

2.Tax exemption for medical expenses:If your employer reimburses your medical expenses, you can claim a tax exemption for the amount received up to a maximum of Rs. 15,000 per year.


Home loan


     Taking a home loan is a common way to purchase a home in India.It also provides certain tax benefits

1.Tax deduction under Section 24: You can claim a tax deduction for the interest paid towards a home loan under Section 24 of the Income Tax Act. The deduction amount is limited to a maximum of Rs. 2 lakh per year for a self-occupied property. For a property that is rented out, there is no limit to the deduction amount.

2.Tax deduction under Section 80C:You can claim a tax deduction for the principal repayment of a home loan under Section 80C of the Income Tax Act. The deduction amount is limited to a maximum of Rs. 1.5 lakh per year.

3.Tax deduction for first-time homebuyers:If you are a first-time homebuyer, you can claim an additional tax deduction of up to Rs. 50,000 per year under Section 80EEA of the Income Tax Act. This deduction is available over and above the deduction available under Section 24 and Section 80C.


Donations to charity


     Donations made to certain charitable organizations are eligible for tax deductions under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act.Benefits in tax due to charity are.

1.Tax deduction under Section 80G:You can claim a tax deduction for donations made to certain charitable organizations under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. The deduction amount is limited to a maximum of 50% or 100% of the donation amount, depending on the nature of the organization and the scheme under which the donation is made.

2.Tax deduction for donations to the Prime Minister's National Relief Fund (PMNRF):Donations made to the PMNRF are eligible for a tax deduction of 100% of the donation amount under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act.

3.Tax exemption for donations to political parties: Donations made to registered political parties are eligible for tax exemption under Section 80GGC of the Income Tax Act. However, this exemption is available only for donations made by individuals and not by companies or firms.


Business expenses


     Every company has its own expanses such as payroll,rent,utilities cost etc.As of income tax guidence business expenses will be subtracted from company's net profit or loss.Company needs to maintain income statement in order to show business expences .The income statement explains the whole picture of your company's expenses and revenues in certain given period of time.The income statement can be categorize in following types:

1.Cost of Product sold:When a company manufactures certain product there is a cost generated and this cost is called cost of product sold .Its deducted from company's total revenue.Cost of product sold includes cost of raw material , labour cost , shipping cost ,sales cost etc.

2.Operating Costs:Operating cost are the expenses that needs to maintenance the business.Operating cost includes marketing cost and compensation .Operating cost are deductable from gross profit.

3.Depreciation:Its a process when company assets decreses over the time and this helps to reduce the tax .

4.Interest expensse:Expenses that are paid in intrest of loans are also deductable from taxable income.
